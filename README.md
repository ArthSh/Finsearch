Checkpoint 1: Introduction to Interest Rates and Monetary Policy

Interest rates represent the cost of borrowing money or the return on investment for lending money. Central banks, such as RBI set these rates to influence the economy. By adjusting interest rates, central banks aim to control inflation,manage employment levels, and stabilize the financial system. Lowering rates typically stimulates economical growth by making borrowing cheaper, while raising rates can make borrowing more expensive. Understanding these fundamentals is essential for analyzing the impact of interest rate changes on financial markets.

Checkpoint 2: Analyze Central Bank's Process to implement interest rate hikes

Interest rate hikes are a tool in a central bank's monetary policy arsenal. These hikes involve increasing the short-term interest rates that banks charge each other for overnight loans. Central banks may raise rates to combat Inflation, cool down an overheated economy, or address financial stability concerns. This section deals with mechanisms through which rate hikes are implemented, the indicators that influence such decisions, and the expected macroeconomic effects.

Checkpoint 3: Impact of interest rate hike on various asset classes

Impact on EQUITY MARKET: Interest rate hikes can significantly affect equity markets. higher interest rates increase borrowing costs for companies, potentially reducing their profitability and growth aspects, which can negatively impact stock prices. Aditionally, higher rates might lead to a shift in investor preference from stocks to bonds, seeking safer returns.

Impact on BOND & DERIVATIVE MARKETS: Bond and derivatives are directly influenced by interest rate changes. When interest rates rise, the prices of existing bonds with lower yields decrease, making them less attractive compared to new bonds issued at higher rates. In derivative markets, interest rate hikes can alter the pricing of options and futures, influencing hedging strategies and risk management.
